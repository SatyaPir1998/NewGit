About the Research

An integrated genome scale metabolic model combines information about the genetic makeup and gene expression of a cell with its metabolic processes. the model focuses on normal human bronchial epithelial cells infected with SARS-CoV-2, the virus responsible for COVID-19. To create the integrated genome scale metabolic model, researchers would start by collecting data on the gene expression of the bronchial epithelial cells during infection with SARS-CoV-2. This involves identifying which genes are activated or suppressed in response to the viral infection. Gene expression data can be obtained through techniques such as RNA sequencing or microarray analysis. Next, the researchers would gather information about the macromolecular makeup of SARS-CoV-2. This includes details about the viral proteins, enzymes, and other macromolecules that play a role in its replication and interaction with the host cell. By integrating the gene expression data of the infected cells with the macromolecular information of the virus, researchers can construct a comprehensive model of the metabolic processes occurring within the infected cells. This model takes into account the biochemical reactions, pathways, and interactions between the host cell and the virus. The integrated genome scale metabolic model allows researchers to simulate and analyze the metabolic behavior of the infected cells. It can provide insights into how the virus alters the metabolic processes of the host cell, potentially leading to changes in nutrient utilization, energy production, and other cellular functions. By studying the model, researchers can gain a better understanding of the host-virus interactions at the metabolic level. This information can be valuable for identifying potential drug targets, developing therapeutic strategies, and improving our understanding of the pathogenesis of viral infections. It's important to note that the creation of such a model is a complex process that requires expertise in molecular biology, bioinformatics, and systems biology. The integration of gene expression data and macromolecular information is challenging but can provide valuable insights into the interplay between viruses and their host cells at a metabolic level.

SARS-CoV-2 alters Lung Cell Metabolism, shows IIT Kharagpur research model > NDTV

“We have used the gene expression of normal human bronchial cells infected with SARS-CoV-2 along with the macromolecular make-up of the virus to create this integrated genome-scale metabolic model. The growth rate predicted by the model showed a very high agreement with experimentally and clinically reported effects of SARS-CoV-2,” said Dr Amit Ghosh, Assistant Professor, School of Energy Science and Engineering, IIT Kharagpur.


IIT Kharagpur model predicts effect of SARS-CoV-2 virus on lungs,post-infection > Firstpost

A model has been developed by Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur researchers predicting alteration in metabolic reaction rates of lung cells post SARS-CoV-2 infection. The research would lead to a better understanding of metabolic reprogramming and aid the development of better therapeutics to deal with the viral pandemics, an institute spokesperson said on Wednesday. The spokesperson said researchers at IIT Kharagpur have, for the first time, reported such a method to find an alteration in metabolic reaction rates inside lung cells when they are affected by virus/pathogens.
